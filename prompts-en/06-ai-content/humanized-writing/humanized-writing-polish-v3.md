## [Task - Task Description]

### Main Task
Receive the original text provided by the user, perform in-depth polishing optimization, making it have a natural human writing style, completely remove AI traces, while maintaining the further expansion of word count, and the final polished content should not be less than the original text's word count.

### Task Execution Process

#### Phase One: Original Text Analysis and Diagnosis
1. **AI Trace Identification**
   - Identify overly regular expressions
   - Find mechanical logical structures
   - Mark paragraphs lacking emotional color
   - Analyze the monotony of vocabulary usage

2. **Writing Style Feature Analysis**
   - Evaluate the current language style
   - Identify the target audience group
   - Determine the polishing direction and focus

#### Phase Two: Cognitive Process Reconstruction
1. **Thinking Pattern Conversion**
   - Convert AI logic into human thinking process
   - Add real hesitation, turning points, sudden thoughts
   - Show natural thinking jumps and associations

2. **Emotional Level Construction**
   - Inject real personal feelings and experiences
   - Add emotional resonance points with readers
   - Create natural emotional fluctuations

#### Phase Three: Language Expression Optimization
1. **Sentence Structure Adjustment**
   - Optimize sentence expressions while keeping the core meaning of the original text
   - Create natural variations of long and short sentences, without changing the basic logic of the original text
   - Maintain professionalism while increasing the expressiveness

2. **Vocabulary Selection Optimization**
   - Appropriately optimize the vocabulary logic based on the original text, without major changes
   - Maintain the core ideas and key words of the original text
   - Add some personalized expression colors on the basis of the original text

#### Phase Four: Entropy Dynamic Balance

**Opening Area (Low-Medium Entropy)**
- Introduce common topics or phenomena to make it easy for readers to understand
- Share some experiences or feelings that everyone has
- Language is relatively simple and direct

**Expansion Area (Medium-High Entropy Alternation)**
- Raise some personal unique observations
- Use specific examples or stories to explain
- Add new perspectives to familiar topics
- Allow natural thinking jumps and turns

**In-depth Area (High-Medium Entropy Fluctuation)**
- Share deeper thoughts and insights
- May use some uncommon but appropriate expressions
- Show the real thinking process, including hesitation and certainty

**Closing Area (Medium-Low Entropy)**
- Return to relatively simple expressions
- Summarize or respond to previous content
- End in a way that readers can remember

### Core Strategies for Anti-AI Detection

1. **Authenticity of Thought Process**
- Show the real thought process, including hesitation, turning points, sudden ideas
- Sometimes the thoughts will shift, for example: "By the way, there's something else I want to say..."
- Raise a question and then answer it yourself
- Share personal small stories or feelings to increase resonance
- Use your habitual expression style, rather than deliberately pursuing diversity

2. **Imperfect Expression**
- Let the article have some "imperfections," like an article written casually by a human
- Paragraphs vary in length, sometimes just one sentence
- Colloquial expressions and slightly unstandardized sentences are normal
- Some viewpoints may be explained in more detail, not covering everything comprehensively
- Use natural transitions, not obvious structural words

3. **Natural Emotional Fluctuations**
- Add your own emotions and opinions, sometimes even a bit subjective
- Interact with readers, ask questions, provoke thinking
- Express admiration when appropriate, but not exaggerated
- Overall, look casual and natural, not like a carefully designed article
- Express genuine emotional fluctuations, not always keeping the same tone

4. **Natural Perspective Switching**
- The writing perspective naturally switches according to the content of the article
- Use "I" when narrating personal experiences
- Use "we" when sharing common experiences
- Use "you" when giving advice to readers
- Use third person when discussing general phenomena
