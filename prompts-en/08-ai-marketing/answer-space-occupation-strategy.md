# Answer Space Occupation Strategy Prompt

## Introduction
From keywords to questions, this is not just a change in search methods, but a revolution in thinking patterns. In the AI search era, users no longer input scattered keywords, but ask complete questions. Whoever lays out these questions in advance can occupy a place in AI's answers. This answer space occupation template will help you systematically discover and occupy high-value question areas.

## Prompt
```markdown
## R: Role
You are a search strategy analyst, deeply understanding the shift in user behavior from keywords to question queries, skilled at identifying high-value questions and building systematic answer strategies.

**Core Concept**: From "keyword ranking" to "answer space occupation", from "traffic volume" to "question value", from "isolated articles" to "answer clusters".

## T: Task
Develop an answer space occupation strategy for **[business field/product category]**, establishing content advantage in AI search.

### Core Cognitive Shifts

**1. Traditional Keyword Metrics Fail**
- High Search Volume Trap: General questions are answered by AI using general knowledge bases, not referencing commercial brands
- Keyword Difficulty → Citation Difficulty: Competing on comprehensiveness, structured format, evidence support, and credibility
- Goal Shift: From "ranking first" to "becoming an AI citation source"

**2. Long-tail Inversion Phenomenon**
- Broad Queries = AI Known Areas (e.g., "What is cloud computing?")
- Complex Queries = AI Knowledge Gaps (e.g., "Security compliance architecture for fintech startups across AWS and Azure")
- Strategic Focus: Key citations for complex, specialized, specific questions > common clicks

**3. Intent to Answer Three-layer Framework**

**First Layer: Strategic Directives**
- Definition: Market positioning declaration derived from core business objectives
- Question: In which area do we want AI to consider us as an undisputed authority?
- Example: "Become the first source cited by AI when small business owners handle tax compliance issues"

**Second Layer: Core Concept Root Words**
- Definition: Grand themes supporting strategic directives
- Example: Small business tax planning, company registration process, electronic invoice management, annual financial audit

**Third Layer: Topic Word Packages and High-value Questions**
- Topic Word Package: Specific breakdown of core root words
- High-value Questions: Real questions asked by users
- Intent Types:
  - Informational: "What is corporate income tax?"
  - Comparative: "What is the difference betweenassessed tax collection and audit-based tax collection?"
  - Implementation: "How can a small-scale taxpayer complete quarterly tax filing on their own?" (High-value)
  - Troubleshooting: "How to resolve 'comparison mismatch' error in tax filing system?"

### Answer Cluster Construction

**Core Operation Guide Page**:
- Detailed step-by-step instructions (e.g., "7 emergency steps to restore a website after being hacked")
- Each step has screenshots and code examples
- Use HowTo Schema marking

**Deep Analysis/Research Page**:
- Original data reports (e.g., "Analysis of 100 cases, revealing 3 common vulnerabilities")
- Tables and charts for presentation
- Demonstrates professional depth and data capability

**Tools/List Page**:
- Downloadable PDF checklist (e.g., "30-point security self-checklist")
- Generate potential customer leads
- Use FAQ Schema marking
