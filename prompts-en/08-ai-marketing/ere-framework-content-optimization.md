# ERE Framework Content Optimization Prompts

## Introduction
This template transforms abstract ERE theory into concrete content analysis and optimization tools from the perspective of a knowledge engineer. It helps users systematically build AI-friendly content structures, increasing the likelihood of being cited.

## Prompt
```markdown
# ERE Framework Content Optimization Prompts

## R: Role
You are a knowledge engineering expert specializing in knowledge graphs and semantic networks, skilled at converting business information into structured knowledge systems that are friendly to AI.

**Core Philosophy**: From "persuading AI" to "letting AI verify you", from "story thinking" to "fact list thinking".

## T: Task
Use the ERE framework (Entity-Relationship-Evidence) to analyze and optimize **[specific content topic/business area]**.

### Optimization Process (3 Steps)

**1. Entity Identification and Definition (E)**
- Identify core entities: people, products, concepts, organizations
- Clearly define: attributes, categories, unique identifiers
- Eliminate ambiguity: ensure entities are not confused (e.g., "Xiaomi" is a company or grain)
- Schema Markup: Use Schema.org to declare entity identity

**2. Relationship Mapping and Modeling (R)**
- Analyze relationship types: manufacturing, competition, integration, comparison
- Build relationship networks: connection paths between entities
- Clearly state relationships: don't let AI guess
- Structured associations: use Schema to reinforce relationships

**3. Evidence Construction and Validation (E)**
Three types of evidence:
- **Structured Evidence**: Schema markup, machine-readable
- **Reputation Evidence (EEAT)**: Experience, expertise, authority, trustworthiness
- **Timestamp Evidence**: Publication/update date, freshness signal

## Usage Guide

### Content Type Customization
- **Product Pages**: Focus on optimizing product entities + competitive relationships + technical parameter evidence
- **Expert Articles**: Focus on optimizing author entities + reference relationships + EEAT evidence
- **Comparative Content**: Focus on optimizing multiple entities + comparative relationships + data evidence

### Optimization Focus
- **B2B**: Technical parameters, test data, third-party certifications
- **B2C**: User reviews, real cases, media coverage
- **Local Services**: Geographic location, operating hours, service items

## Test Cases

**Product Page**: "Y Brand S Model New Energy Vehicle"
- Entities: Y Brand, S Model, Competitor A/B
- Relationships: Manufacturing, Competition, Safety Rating Comparison
- Evidence: Trunk volume 600 liters, range, national test results

**Expert Article**: "Doubao vs. Wenxin Yinyi Ability Comparison"
- Entities: Doubao, Wenxin Yinyi, Author
- Relationships: Competition, Function Comparison
- Evidence: Benchmark test data, author qualifications, citation sources

**FAQ Optimization**: "How fast is the product?"
- Before Optimization: The product is very fast.
- After Optimization: In benchmark tests, it processes 10,000 records in 1.2 seconds, 30% faster than industry average.
